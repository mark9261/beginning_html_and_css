Since Its almost Halloween I want to talk some about it

Halloween originated from the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain, celebrated around November 1st to mark the end of summer and the harvest. The Celts believed this was a time when the boundary between the worlds of the living and the dead blurred, and ghosts of the dead returned to Earth. To ward off spirits, they would light bonfires and wear costumes. The holiday was later influenced by Christianity, with the evening before All Saints' Day becoming known as All Hallows' Eve, or Halloween.

How its changed

Halloween has changed majorly over the years, what used to be a ancient festival has now turned into a night where children dress up in costumms and go house to ask for candy. This tradition has occourd due to traditions that combined ancient practices of disguising oneself and appeasing spirits with later customs of "souling" and "guising". Initially, costumes were worn to ward off or blend in with spirits, while the tradition of asking for food or treats evolved from the "souling" practice, where people would pray for the dead in exchange for food gifts. Over time, this evolved into the modern "trick-or-treating," where the threat of a trick is exchanged for a treat, often candy.
